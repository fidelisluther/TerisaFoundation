INITIATIVES

Our mission is to provide support, resources, and education to underserved communities, with a particular focus on women and girls. We recognize that women and girls face unique challenges and barriers, and we are committed to addressing these challenges head-on.

Pad A Girl Initiative

The Pad A Girl Initiative aims to address a critical issue affecting girls in underserved communities - the inability to afford sanitary pads. For many young girls, the onset of menstruation is a defining moment in their lives, marking the transition from childhood to womanhood. However, the inability to access affordable sanitary pads often denies them this experience and leads to adverse consequences such as dropping out of school, engaging in risky behaviors, and being stigmatized.

The Pad A Girl Initiative seeks to address this issue by providing sanitary pads to girls in underserved communities. By doing so, the initiative aims to help these girls stay in school, reduce stigma and shame associated with menstruation, and empower them to reach their full potential. The initiative also provides education on menstrual hygiene and health, which is crucial for the wellbeing of these girls.

The need for the Pad A Girl Initiative cannot be overstated. According to recent statistics, over 65% of girls in Kenya are unable to access sanitary pads, leading to adverse consequences such as increased school dropout rates, reduced academic performance, and increased risk of infection. By providing access to sanitary pads, the initiative is helping to break down barriers to education and empower girls to realize their full potential.

The impact of the Pad A Girl Initiative has been significant. Since its inception, the initiative has reached over 10,000 girls in underserved communities and provided them with the necessary sanitary pads to help them stay in school. Many of these girls have gone on to excel in their academic pursuits and pursue their dreams, which would have been impossible without the initiative.

You can get involved with the Pad A Girl Initiative by donating money or sanitary pads. Your support will go a long way in empowering girls in underserved communities and enabling them to reach their full potential. By supporting this initiative, you will be making a difference in the lives of young girls and helping to break down barriers to education and empowerment.

The Tech Space Initiative

The Tech Space Initiative is an exciting new program aimed at introducing high school students to the world of technology. The program seeks to equip students with basic computer skills and knowledge in topics such as cyber security, IoT, coding, computer packages, and an introduction to computers. The program is open to students within the age range of 14-18 years old.

The Tech Space Initiative is crucial as it seeks to bridge the digital divide by providing access to technology and equipping students with the necessary skills to succeed in the modern world. With technology rapidly transforming the job market, it is critical that students are equipped with the skills needed to compete in the 21st century workplace. Additionally, the initiative aims to inspire and encourage students to consider careers in technology, which is a rapidly growing field with endless possibilities.

The program will be delivered through a combination of lectures, hands-on workshops, and practical exercises. Students will have access to state-of-the-art equipment and software, ensuring that they receive a world-class education. The topics covered in the program will be tailored to the needs of the students, ensuring that they receive the necessary knowledge to succeed in the digital age.

Getting involved with the Tech Space Initiative is easy. If you are passionate about technology and education, you can volunteer to teach or mentor students in the program. Additionally, you can donate equipment such as laptops, tablets, software or funds, to ensure that the students have access to the necessary tools to succeed. By getting involved with the initiative, you will be making a difference in the lives of young students and helping to bridge the digital divide. Together, we can ensure that every student has access to the tools and skills needed to succeed in the modern world.

Biashara Initiative

Micro businesses are small enterprises typically run by one or two people, often in underserved communities where larger businesses are less common. These businesses are crucial in providing livelihoods and essential goods and services to local communities. They also play a vital role in building resilient local economies and creating jobs.

The Biashara Initiative is an organization that provides training, mentorship, and grants/investments to support micro-businesses in underserved communities. By equipping women aged 20-35 years with the necessary skills to run successful micro-businesses, the initiative aims to promote economic growth and reduce poverty.

Through the Biashara Initiative, many businesses have been able to grow and provide better services to their communities. For example, Lucy started a small grocery shop in her community but struggled to attract customers. After receiving training and mentorship from the Biashara Initiative, she was able to improve her business practices and attract more customers. With a grant from the initiative, she was able to expand her business and provide more products to her customers. This not only increased her income but also provided essential goods to the community.

People can support the Biashara Initiative in various ways. By donating money, individuals can help to fund the training and mentorship programs that are essential to the success of micro-businesses. Additionally, investing in businesses through the initiative provides capital that businesses can use to expand and grow. By supporting the Biashara Initiative, individuals can make a significant impact on the lives of women entrepreneurs and their communities.
